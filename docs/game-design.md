# Game design summary

## Core loop

The player expands a logistics network, builds production facilities, transports goods, supplies cities, and automates regional distribution through depots.

## Network

- Real-world map
- Swiss cities and towns
- Roads follow plausible real routes
- Multiple road classes and capacities
- Shared road sections and automatic junctions
- Rail infrastructure

## Economy

- Construction costs
- Vehicle purchase, maintenance, and distance costs
- Factory construction and maintenance
- City demand and daily consumption
- Production chains and intermediate goods

## Operations

- Manual dispatching
- Multi-stop routes
- Per-stop unloading rules
- Daily recurring schedules
- Depot-specific vehicles
- Automatic mixed-cargo delivery rounds
- Automatic replenishment from production facilities
- Calendar-style daily timetable

## Current product chains include

- Agricultural raw goods
- Fresh fruit and vegetables
- Meat and refrigerated goods
- Timber and industrial goods
- Canned tomatoes, corn, and peas
- Ravioli variants

## Long-term design goal

Reduce repetitive vehicle micromanagement while preserving meaningful logistics decisions. The player should design and optimise a system rather than manually trigger every truck movement.
